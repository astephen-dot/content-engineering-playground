# **Blog Type Guide (Discovery\_Webinar)**

## **Webinar Follow-Up**

Version: 1.0

### **Classification**

```
Content Type: Blog

Buyer Stage:
  - Discovery

Blog Type:
  - Webinar Follow-Up
```

---

# **Purpose**

A Webinar Follow-Up blog transforms webinar discussions into a structured, searchable, and reusable content asset.

The goal is not to summarize the webinar minute-by-minute.

The goal is not to create a transcript.

The goal is to help readers understand:

* Key ideas discussed  
* Expert insights  
* Emerging trends  
* Recommended approaches

The reader should leave saying:

"I understand the important takeaways without needing to watch the webinar."

---

Use this template when source material comes from:

### **Webinars**

* Customer webinar

* Product webinar  
* Partner webinar

### **Virtual Events**

Examples:

* Roundtables  
* Executive discussions

* Fireside chats

---

### **Conference Sessions**

Examples:

* Talent Summit  
* HR Conference  
* AI Governance Forum  
* Public Sector Event

---

# **Audience Mindset**

The audience is asking:

```
What were the important takeaways?

What did experts discuss?

What should I learn from this?

How does this apply to my organization?
```

---

# **Content Objective**

The article should:

✓ Extract key insights

✓ Organize discussion themes

✓ Highlight expert perspectives

✓ Provide context

✓ Extend webinar value

The article should NOT:

✗ Read like meeting notes

✗ Become a transcript

✗ Capture every discussion point

✗ Become product-heavy

---

# **Webinar Content Selection Rules**

Prioritize:

```
Strategic Insights: High

Industry Trends: High

Research Findings: High

Expert Opinions: High

Audience Questions: Medium

Product Demonstrations: Low

Housekeeping Information: Exclude
```

---

# **Article Structure**

## **H1 Title**

### **Goal**

Focus on the insight, not the webinar itself.

---

### **Preferred Format**

```
Key Takeaways From [Topic]

What We Learned About [Topic]

Insights From Industry Leaders On [Topic]

Lessons From Our [Topic] Discussion
```

---

### **Avoid**

```
Webinar Recap

Webinar Summary

Event Highlights
```

These sound archival rather than educational.

---

### **Examples**

```
Key Takeaways From Our AI Governance Discussion

What We Learned About Skills-Based Organizations

Insights From Talent Leaders On Workforce Intelligence
```

---

# **Section 1**

## **The Big Idea**

### **Goal**

Present the most important takeaway.

---

### **Questions**

```

What idea appeared repeatedly?

What insight framed the discussion?
```

---

### **Rule**

Lead with the strongest insight.

Not the webinar agenda.

# **CTA Guidance**

The webinar itself is often the CTA.

### **Recommended**

```
Watch Webinar Recording

Download Presentation

Read Related Research

Explore Framework

View Additional Resources
```

---

### **Secondary CTA**

```
Read Related Blog

Download Guide

Executive Brief
```

---

### **Avoid**

```
Book Demo

Talk To Sales

Pricing Request
```

Unless the webinar itself was explicitly product-focused.

---

# **Evidence Requirements**

Preferred sources:

```
Speaker Quotes

Panel Insights

Research Referenced During Webinar

Audience Questions

Approved Eightfold Research
```

---

# **Speaker Attribution Rule**

When citing experts:

### **Good**

```
According to [Speaker Name], organizations are increasingly...
```

### **Bad**

```
The webinar stated...
```

People create authority.

Events do not.

---

# **Eightfold POV Placement**

Use:

```
Industry Context

↓

Webinar Insight

↓

Expert Perspectives

↓

Business Implications

↓

Eightfold Perspective

↓

Additional Resources
```

The webinar should remain the primary source of authority.

✓ Expert perspectives highlighted

✓ Business implications included

✓ Audience questions considered

✓ Webinar value extended
