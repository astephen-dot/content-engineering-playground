# **Blog Type Guide (Awareness-Problem Identification)**

## **Problem Identification**

Version: 1.0

### **Classification**

```
Content Type: Blog

Buyer Stage:
  - Awareness

Blog Type:
  - Problem Identification
```

---

# **Purpose**

A Problem Identification blog helps readers recognize a business challenge they may be experiencing but have not yet fully defined.

The goal is not to present a solution.

The goal is not to discuss products.

The goal is to help the audience understand:

* What is happening  
* Why it is happening  
* Why it matters

The reader should leave saying:

"That's exactly what we're dealing with."

---

# **When To Use**

Use this template when the request is focused on:

### **Symptoms**

Examples:

* Hiring is slowing down  
* Attrition is increasing  
* Workforce planning feels reactive  
* AI adoption is creating uncertainty  
* Recruiters are overwhelmed

---

### **Emerging Challenges**

Examples:

* Skills shortages  
* Internal mobility gaps  
* Compliance risk  
* Workforce visibility issues  
* Candidate experience challenges

---

# **Audience Mindset**

At this stage the audience is not evaluating vendors.

They are trying to understand:

```
Why is this happening?

How serious is this?

What is causing it?

What happens if nothing changes?
```

---

# **Content Objective**

The article should:

✓ Name the problem

✓ Explain root causes

✓ Demonstrate business impact

✓ Reframe existing assumptions

✓ Establish urgency

The article should NOT:

✗ Compare vendors

✗ Discuss implementation

✗ Dive into product capabilities

✗ Lead with Eightfold

---

# **Article Structure**

---

## **H1 Title**

### **Goal**

Clearly articulate the challenge.

### **Formula**

```
Why [Challenge] Is Becoming A Problem For [Audience]

The Hidden Cost Of [Problem]

What's Really Causing [Challenge]
```

### **Examples**

```
Why Traditional Hiring Models Are Breaking Down

The Hidden Cost Of Workforce Blindness

What's Really Causing Recruiter Burnout
```

---

# **Introduction**

## **Goal**

Create recognition.

The audience should immediately identify with the issue.

---

## **Structure**

### **Paragraph 1**

Describe a familiar symptom.

---

### **Paragraph 2**

Explain why it matters.

---

### **Paragraph 3**

Connect the symptom to a larger trend.

---

### **Paragraph 4**

Preview what the article explores.

---

## **Reader Test**

The reader should think:

"This sounds familiar."

---

# **Section 1**

## **What Is Happening?**

### **Goal**

Clearly define the problem.

### **Questions**

```
What is changing?

What challenge are organizations experiencing?

Why are leaders talking about it?
```

### **Include**

* Industry observations  
* Market shifts  
* Operational realities

---

# **Section 2**

## **Why Is It Happening?**

### **Goal**

Explain root causes.

### **Questions**

```
What forces created this challenge?

What assumptions no longer hold?

What has changed?
```

### **Include**

* Technology shifts  
* Workforce changes  
* Economic pressures  
* Regulatory changes

---

# **Section 3**

## **Why Traditional Approaches Are Struggling**

### **Goal**

Challenge conventional thinking.

### **Questions**

```
Why aren't current methods working?

What limitations are becoming visible?
```

### **Include**

* Process limitations  
* Organizational constraints  
* Legacy systems  
* Outdated operating models

---

# **Section 4**

## **The Business Impact**

### **Goal**

Demonstrate consequences.

### **Categories**

* Cost  
* Productivity  
* Risk  
* Retention  
* Candidate Experience  
* Agility

### **Questions**

```
What happens if the problem continues?

Who is affected?

How does it impact outcomes?
```

---

# **Section 5**

## **What Leading Organizations Are Recognizing**

### **Goal**

Introduce a new perspective.

Not a solution.

A shift in thinking.

### **Questions**

```
How are forward-thinking organizations viewing this challenge?

What patterns are emerging?
```

### **Include**

* Industry evolution  
* Strategic mindset shifts  
* New approaches gaining traction

---

# **Conclusion**

## **Goal**

Leave the reader with a new understanding.

---

## **Structure**

### **Recap**

Problem

### **Reframe**

New perspective

### **Forward Look**

Why this matters moving forward

# **CTA Guidance**

Use low-friction CTAs only.

### **Recommended**

```

Watch Webinar

Read Related Article
```

### **Avoid**

```

Book Meeting

Start Pilot

Talk To Sales
```

# **Evidence Requirements**

Preferred sources:

```
Industry Reports

Analyst Research

Market Trends

Customer Observations

SME Interviews

Approved Eightfold Research
```

---

# **Eightfold POV Placement**

Do not introduce Eightfold immediately.

Use this progression:

```
Problem

↓

Root Cause

↓

Business Impact

↓

Industry Shift

↓

Eightfold Perspective

↓

Future Outlook
```

The Eightfold POV should appear naturally as an observation about the market, not as a product pitch.

---

# **Draft Quality Checklist**

Before publishing:

✓ Problem is clearly defined

✓ Reader can recognize the challenge

✓ Root causes explained

✓ Business impact demonstrated

✓ Industry shift introduced

✓ No product-heavy messaging

✓ No competitive comparisons

✓ Evidence supports claims

✓ CTA is educational

✓ Content aligns with Awareness stage
