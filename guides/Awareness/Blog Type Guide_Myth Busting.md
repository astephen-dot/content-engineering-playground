---

# **Purpose**

A Myth-Busting blog challenges commonly held assumptions, misconceptions, or outdated beliefs.

The goal is not to prove Eightfold is right.

The goal is to help readers rethink conventional wisdom.

The reader should leave saying:

"I hadn't thought about it that way."

or

"That assumption may no longer be true."

---

# **When To Use**

Use this template when:

### **Market Misconceptions Exist**

Examples:

* AI increases bias  
* Internal mobility hurts productivity  
* Skills-based hiring is impossible to scale  
* Workforce planning requires more data  
* Recruiters will be replaced by AI

---

### **Industry Narratives Need Reframing**

Examples:

* More recruiters solve hiring bottlenecks  
* AI governance slows innovation  
* Career pathing only matters for retention  
* Workforce intelligence is just reporting

---

# **Audience Mindset**

The audience currently believes something.

The article exists to challenge that belief.

---

### **Current Belief**

```
This is how things work.
```

---

### **Desired Outcome**

```
Maybe the reality is more nuanced.
```

---

# **Content Objective**

The article should:

✓ Identify a common belief

✓ Explain why it exists

✓ Examine evidence

✓ Reveal limitations

✓ Introduce a better perspective

The article should NOT:

✗ Attack the audience

✗ Mock existing thinking

✗ Create controversy for attention

✗ Turn into product promotion

---

# **Article Structure**

## **H1 Title**

### **Goal**

Challenge an assumption.

---

### **Formula**

```
The Myth Of [Belief]

Why [Common Belief] Is Wrong

The Truth About [Topic]

What Most People Get Wrong About [Topic]
```

---

### **Examples**

```
The Myth That AI Creates More Hiring Bias

What Most Organizations Get Wrong About Internal Mobility

Why More Recruiters Won't Fix Hiring Bottlenecks

The Truth About Workforce Intelligence
```

---

# **Introduction**

## **Goal**

Surface the myth immediately.

---

## **Structure**

### **Paragraph 1**

State the common belief.

---

### **Paragraph 2**

Explain why people believe it.

---

### **Paragraph 3**

Introduce tension.

Hint that the reality is more complex.

---

### **Paragraph 4**

Preview the article.

---

# **Section 1**

## **The Myth**

### **Goal**

Define the belief clearly.

---

### **Questions**

```
What do people believe?

Why has this belief become common?

Where did it originate?
```

---

### **Include**

* Industry assumptions  
* Historical context  
* Common narratives

---

# **Section 2**

## **Why The Myth Persists**

### **Goal**

Explain why the misconception survives.

---

### **Questions**

```
Why does this idea feel true?

What reinforces it?

What experiences support it?
```

---

### **Include**

* Legacy practices  
* Organizational habits  
* Market narratives  
* Human biases

---

# **Section 3**

## **What The Evidence Shows**

### **Goal**

Challenge the assumption.

---

### **Questions**

```
What does the data suggest?

What are we overlooking?

Where does the assumption break down?
```

---

### **Include**

* Research  
* Industry examples  
* Expert insights  
* Market observations

---

# **Section 4**

## **A Better Way To Think About It**

### **Goal**

Replace the myth with a stronger perspective.

---

### **Questions**

```
What should leaders understand instead?

What changes when we view the problem differently?
```

---

### **Include**

* Emerging thinking  
* Strategic frameworks  
* Industry shifts

---

# **Section 5**

## **What Leaders Should Do Next**

### **Goal**

Translate insight into action.

---

### **Questions**

```
How should leaders respond?

What questions should they ask?

What should they reconsider?
```

---

### **Include**

* Reflection prompts  
* Strategic recommendations  
* Areas for exploration
