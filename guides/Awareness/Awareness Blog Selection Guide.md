# **Awareness Blog Selection Guide**

Version: 1.0

---

# **Purpose**

This guide helps writers, editors, AI systems, and workflow automations select the correct **Awareness Blog Type** before content creation begins.

The objective is to ensure that content is aligned to the reader's intent and the buyer journey rather than simply matching a topic.

---

# **Awareness Stage Definition**

## **Reader Mindset**

At the Awareness stage, readers are not actively evaluating solutions.

They are attempting to understand:

```
What is happening?

Why is it happening?

Why should I care?

What should I pay attention to?
```

---

## **Content Goals**

Awareness content should help readers:

* Understand a challenge  
* Recognize a trend  
* Reconsider assumptions  
* Interpret evidence  
* Understand strategic implications

Awareness content should NOT focus on:

* Product evaluation  
* Vendor comparison  
* Feature explanation  
* Implementation guidance  
* Purchase decisions

---

# **Blog Type Decision Tree**

## **Question 1**

Is the content primarily about a challenge or pain point?

### **YES**

Use:

→ Problem Identification

### **Example**

```
Why Recruiters Are Struggling With Hiring Volume
```

---

## **Question 2**

Is the content primarily about a market, technology, workforce, or regulatory shift?

### **YES**

Use:

→ Industry Trend

### **Example**

```
The Rise Of Agentic AI In Recruiting
```

---

## **Question 3**

Is the content primarily challenging a common belief or misconception?

### **YES**

Use:

→ Myth-Busting

### **Example**

```
The Myth That AI Replaces Recruiters
```

---

## **Question 4**

Is the content primarily written for leaders and focused on business implications?

### **YES**

Use:

→ Executive Perspective

### **Example**

```
Why Workforce Agility Has Become A Leadership Priority
```

---

## **Question 5**

Is the content primarily based on research, surveys, benchmarks, or data findings?

### **YES**

Use:

→ Research-Led

### **Example**

```
What The Data Says About Internal Mobility
```

---

# **Selection Matrix**

| If the request is asking... | Use |
| ----- | ----- |
| What problem exists? | Problem Identification |
| What trend is emerging? | Industry Trend |
| What assumption should be challenged? | Myth-Busting |
| What should leaders care about? | Executive Perspective |
| What does the evidence show? | Research-Led |

# **Quick Reference Guide**

## **Problem Identification**

### **Purpose**

Help readers recognize a challenge.

### **Starts With**

```
There is a problem.
```

### **Reader Takeaway**

```
We are experiencing this challenge.
```

### **Common Triggers**

* Hiring bottlenecks  
* Skills shortages  
* Retention challenges  
* Workforce visibility issues  
* Compliance concerns

---

## **Industry Trend**

### **Purpose**

Explain a market shift.

### **Starts With**

```
Something is changing.
```

### **Reader Takeaway**

```
This trend is becoming important.
```

### **Common Triggers**

* Agentic AI  
* Skills-based organizations  
* Workforce intelligence  
* Regulatory changes  
* Market evolution

---

## **Myth-Busting**

### **Purpose**

Challenge assumptions.

### **Starts With**

```
Many people believe...
```

### **Reader Takeaway**

```
That belief may not be accurate.
```

### **Common Triggers**

* AI misconceptions  
* Hiring myths  
* Internal mobility myths  
* Workforce planning misconceptions

---

## **Executive Perspective**

### **Purpose**

Highlight strategic implications.

### **Starts With**

```
Leaders should pay attention to...
```

### **Reader Takeaway**

```
This requires executive focus.
```

### **Common Triggers**

* CHRO priorities  
* CIO priorities  
* Board-level concerns  
* Business transformation  
* Organizational capability

---

## **Research-Led**

### **Purpose**

Interpret evidence.

### **Starts With**

```
Research shows...
```

### **Reader Takeaway**

```
The findings are significant.
```

### **Common Triggers**

* Surveys  
* Benchmark reports  
* Industry studies  
* Analyst research  
* Internal research

# **AI Routing Rules**

When classifying a request:

## **Priority Order**

```
1. Research-Led
2. Myth-Busting
3. Executive Perspective
4. Industry Trend
5. Problem Identification
```

### **Why?**

Because research, myths, and executive angles are often explicitly stated in requests.

A request such as:

```
Based on a recent survey, explore why leaders struggle with AI governance.
```

Should be:

```
Research-Led
```

not

```
Problem Identification
```

because the evidence source dictates the structure.

# **Final Validation**

Before selecting a template ask:

### **What is the article trying to accomplish?**

| Goal | Template |
| ----- | ----- |
| Define a challenge | Problem Identification |
| Explain a shift | Industry Trend |
| Challenge a belief | Myth-Busting |
| Interpret strategic implications | Executive Perspective |
| Interpret evidence | Research-Led |
