## **Proof & Results**

Version: 1.0

### **Classification**

```
Content Type: Blog

Buyer Stage:
  - Buy

Blog Type:
  - Proof & Results
```

---

# **Purpose**

A Proof & Results blog provides evidence that a solution, initiative, or approach delivers measurable business outcomes.

Unlike a Customer Story, which focuses on a single organization's experience, Proof & Results content synthesizes evidence across multiple organizations, studies, benchmarks, or outcomes.

The goal is to answer:

Does this actually work?

The reader should leave saying:

"There is credible evidence that organizations achieve meaningful outcomes with this approach."

---

# **When To Use**

Use this template when discussing:

### **Proven Business Outcomes**

Examples:

* Internal mobility improvements  
* Workforce visibility gains  
* Recruiting efficiency  
* Skills intelligence outcomes  
* AI governance maturity

---

### **Research-Based Findings**

Examples:

* Industry benchmarks  
* Customer outcome studies  
* Adoption research  
* Workforce transformation findings

---

### **Aggregated Evidence**

Examples:

* Cross-customer outcomes  
* Program results  
* Market trends  
* Analyst findings

---

# **Audience Mindset**

The audience is asking:

```
What evidence exists?

What outcomes have others achieved?

Is there proof this works?

How do organizations measure success?
```

---

# **Content Objective**

The article should:

✓ Provide evidence

✓ Demonstrate measurable outcomes

✓ Build confidence

✓ Reduce perceived risk

✓ Validate investment decisions

The article should NOT:

✗ Become a customer story

✗ Present unsupported claims

✗ Overstate results

✗ Ignore context

✗ Become product marketing

---

# **Proof & Results Integrity Rules**

## **Evidence First**

Bad:

```
Our solution delivers incredible results.
```

Good:

```
Research and customer outcomes indicate organizations commonly experience...
```

---

## **Show Patterns, Not Anecdotes**

Bad:

```
One customer experienced success.
```

Good:

```
Multiple organizations reported similar improvements.
```

---

## **Include Limitations**

Every article should acknowledge:

```
Context

Assumptions

Variability

Dependencies
```

This strengthens trust.

---

## **Focus On Outcomes**

Prioritize:

```
Business Results

Operational Improvements

Strategic Benefits
```

Over:

```
Features

Screenshots

Product Releases
```

↓

Implementation Story
↓

Proof & Results
↓
What evidence supports success?
```

The key distinction is:

```
Customer Story
= One organization's proof

Proof & Results
= Aggregated proof across many organizations
