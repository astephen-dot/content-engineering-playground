# **Blog Type Guide (Buy\_Customer Story)**

## **Customer Story**

Version: 1.0

### **Classification**

```
Content Type: Blog

Buyer Stage:
  - Buy

Blog Type:
  - Customer Story
```

---

# **Purpose**

A Customer Story demonstrates how a real organization addressed a business challenge and achieved measurable outcomes.

The goal is not to tell a product story.

The goal is not to list features.

The goal is to show:

* The challenge  
* The journey  
* The decisions  
* The outcomes  
* The lessons learned

The reader should leave saying:

"Organizations like ours have successfully solved this problem."

---

# **When To Use**

Use this template when discussing:

### **Customer Success**

Examples:

* Skills Transformation  
* Internal Mobility Programs  
* Workforce Intelligence Adoption  
* AI Governance Initiatives  
* Agentic Hiring Programs

---

### **Business Outcomes**

Examples:

* Reduced time-to-hire  
* Increased internal mobility  
* Improved workforce visibility  
* Higher employee retention  
* Better workforce planning

---

# **Audience Mindset**

The audience is asking:

```
Has anyone like us done this?

What results did they achieve?

What challenges did they encounter?

What can we learn from them?
```

---

# **Content Objective**

The article should:

✓ Build trust

✓ Demonstrate real-world application

✓ Show measurable outcomes

✓ Highlight lessons learned

✓ Reduce perceived risk

The article should NOT:

✗ Become a product walkthrough

✗ Focus primarily on features

✗ Read like a press release

✗ Ignore implementation challenges

✗ Exaggerate results

---

# **Introduction**

## **Goal**

Provide a concise summary of the story.

---

## **Structure**

### **Paragraph 1**

Introduce the customer.

---

### **Paragraph 2**

Describe the challenge.

---

### **Paragraph 3**

Preview the solution approach.

---

### **Paragraph 4**

Highlight outcomes.

---

# **Section 1**

## **The Challenge**

### **Goal**

Establish context.

---

### **Questions**

```
What business problem existed?

Why was it important?

What risks or limitations were present?

What triggered change?
```

---

### **Include**

```
Business Context

Organizational Goals

Operational Challenges

Strategic Drivers
```

---

# **Section 2**

## **Why Change Was Necessary**

### **Goal**

Create urgency.

---

### **Questions**

```
Why couldn't the organization continue as before?

What pressures existed?

What opportunities were being missed?
```

---

### **Include**

```
Business Pressures

Market Changes

Workforce Challenges

Growth Requirements
```

---

# **Section 3**

## **The Approach**

### **Goal**

Explain the strategy.

---

### **Questions**

```
What path did the organization choose?

Why was that approach selected?

Who was involved?
```

---

### **Focus On**

```
Decision-Making

Strategy

Planning

Stakeholder Alignment
```

---

### **Avoid**

Deep product configuration details.

---

# **Section 4**

## **The Implementation Journey**

### **Goal**

Show how change happened.

---

### **Questions**

```
What steps were taken?

What challenges emerged?

How were obstacles addressed?
```

---

### **Include**

```
Key Milestones

Change Management

Stakeholder Engagement

Lessons Learned
```

---

# **Section 5**

## **Outcomes And Impact**

### **Goal**

Demonstrate value.

---

### **Questions**

```
What improved?

How was success measured?

What business outcomes emerged?
```

---

### **Possible Categories**

```

Hiring Outcomes

Retention

Employee Experience

Workforce Visibility
```

### **Preferred Evidence**

# **Section 6**

## **Key Lessons Learned**

### **Goal**

Provide transferable insights.

---

### **Questions**

```
What would the organization do differently?

What advice would they give others?

What contributed most to success?
```

---

### **Rule**

This section should be educational.

Not promotional.

---

# **Customer Story Integrity Rules**

## **Customer Is The Hero**

Bad:

```
Eightfold helped the customer do X.
```

Good:

```
The organization pursued a workforce transformation initiative and...
```

---

## **Focus On Business Outcomes**

Prioritize:

```
Business Impact

Organizational Change

Workforce Outcomes

Leadership Decisions
```

Over:

```
Features

Product Screens

Technical Details
```

---

## **Include Challenges**

Bad:

```
Everything worked perfectly.
```

Good:

```
The organization encountered adoption and change-management challenges that required...
```

Real stories include obstacles.

---

## **Lessons Must Be Transferable**

Readers should be able to learn from the experience.

---

# **Draft Quality Checklist**

Before publishing:

✓ Customer challenge clearly defined

✓ Business context established

✓ Change journey explained

✓ Outcomes documented

✓ Lessons learned included

✓ Customer remains central

✓ Results supported with evidence

✓ Buy-stage alignment maintained

✓ Reader gains actionable insight
