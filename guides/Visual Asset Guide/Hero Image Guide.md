# **Hero Image Guide**

**Version:** V1.0  
**Purpose:** Knowledge Guide  
**Stage:** Post Content Generation → Hero Image Specification

---

# **Overview**

The Hero Image Guide defines how editorial cover images should be planned for long-form content.

A Hero Image is the primary visual representation of an article. Its purpose is to capture attention, establish context, reinforce the article's theme, and encourage readers to engage with the content.

Unlike supporting visuals, a Hero Image is **not intended to explain information**. It functions as an editorial storytelling asset rather than an informational graphic.

This guide determines **what the Hero Image should communicate**, not how it should be generated.

---

# **Core Principles**

Every Hero Image should:

* Introduce the topic visually.  
* Establish the article's tone.  
* Reinforce the central theme.  
* Create curiosity.  
* Support the brand's visual identity.  
* Remain visually simple and memorable.

A Hero Image should never attempt to summarize the entire article.

---

# **Editorial Objectives**

Each Hero Image should communicate **one primary objective**.

| Objective | Description |
| ----- | ----- |
| Introduce | Visually introduce the subject matter |
| Contextualize | Place the reader within a recognizable environment or industry |
| Humanize | Focus on people, experiences, or interactions |
| Inspire | Create aspiration or emotional engagement |
| Conceptualize | Represent abstract ideas through metaphor or symbolism |

Only one objective should be selected.

---

# **Step 1 — Identify the Core Narrative**

Determine the single strongest narrative the Hero Image should communicate.

Ask:

* What is the article fundamentally about?  
* What is the emotional takeaway?  
* What is the first impression readers should have?  
* What visual story can communicate this without relying on text?

---

# **Step 2 — Identify the Primary Subject**

Every Hero Image should have a clear subject.

Possible subjects include:

### **People**

* Recruiters  
* Candidates  
* Managers  
* Employees  
* Executives  
* Teams

---

### **Technology**

* AI systems  
* Digital interfaces  
* Automation  
* Connected platforms  
* Data ecosystems

---

### **Environment**

* Workplace  
* Office  
* Hybrid work  
* Remote work  
* Enterprise environments  
* Learning environments

---

### **Conceptual Elements**

* Growth  
* Opportunity  
* Collaboration  
* Connection  
* Intelligence  
* Transformation

Only one primary subject should dominate the composition.

---

# **Step 3 — Determine the Visual Style**

Select the style that best supports the narrative.

Approved styles:

* Editorial Illustration  
* Conceptual Illustration  
* Minimal Illustration  
* Realistic Composite  
* Stylized 3D Illustration  
* Abstract Editorial Artwork

The selected style should remain consistent with the organization's visual identity.

---

# **Step 4 — Define the Emotional Tone**

Choose the emotional tone.

Examples:

* Optimistic  
* Professional  
* Confident  
* Innovative  
* Human-centered  
* Trustworthy  
* Collaborative  
* Aspirational  
* Calm

Avoid conflicting emotional signals.

---

# **Step 5 — Define the Composition**

Specify the overall composition.

Consider:

### **Focal Point**

What immediately attracts attention?

---

### **Visual Hierarchy**

Which elements should readers notice first?

---

### **Perspective**

Possible options:

* Eye level  
* Isometric  
* Overhead  
* Close-up  
* Wide angle

---

### **Depth**

* Flat composition  
* Moderate depth  
* Cinematic depth

---

### **Negative Space**

Reserve sufficient uncluttered space for:

* Article title  
* Branding  
* Responsive layouts

---

# **Step 6 — Define the Environment**

Specify where the subject exists.

Examples:

* Enterprise office  
* Digital workspace  
* Abstract technological environment  
* Conference setting  
* Remote collaboration  
* Modern workplace

The environment should support—not compete with—the subject.

---

# **Step 7 — Define Supporting Visual Elements**

Supporting elements may include:

* Devices  
* Data streams  
* Documents  
* Icons  
* Abstract shapes  
* Architectural elements  
* Light effects

Supporting elements should reinforce the narrative rather than distract from it.

---

# **Step 8 — Define Exclusions**

Hero Images should avoid:

* Large blocks of readable text  
* Flowcharts  
* Timelines  
* Tables  
* Dense UI screenshots  
* Clip-art aesthetics  
* Generic stock-photo poses  
* Excessive visual clutter  
* Overly literal representations of every article detail

---

# **Step 9 — Accessibility Considerations**

Ensure the Hero Image:

* Maintains clear visual contrast.  
* Supports responsive layouts.  
* Preserves focal points on different aspect ratios.  
* Remains understandable without captions.  
* Allows descriptive alternative text.

---

# **Hero Image Quality Checklist**

Before approving a Hero Image, verify:

* Does it communicate one clear idea?  
* Does it establish the correct editorial tone?  
* Is the primary subject immediately recognizable?  
* Is there a strong focal point?  
* Is there sufficient negative space for title placement?  
* Does the image avoid unnecessary complexity?  
* Does it support accessibility?  
* Does it reinforce the article rather than duplicate it?

# **Output Format**

Every Hero Image recommendation should produce a standardized **Hero Image Specification**.

```
Hero Image

Required: Yes

Editorial Objective:
Contextualize

Primary Narrative:
AI-powered talent intelligence helping recruiters identify potential.

Primary Subject:
Recruiter collaborating with an AI-enabled hiring platform.

Visual Style:
Editorial Illustration

Emotional Tone:
Professional
Innovative
Human-centered

Environment:
Modern enterprise workplace

Composition:
Wide landscape
Single focal point
Balanced negative space
Moderate depth

Supporting Elements:
- Subtle AI visual cues
- Digital connectivity
- Modern workspace

Exclusions:
- No readable text
- No charts
- No screenshots
- No excessive visual clutter

Reason:
The image introduces the article's theme while creating immediate editorial context without attempting to explain the article's detailed content.
```
