# **Visual Prompt Standards**

**Version:** V2.0  
**Purpose:** Execution Guide  
**Stage:** Visual Specification → Prompt Generation

---

# **Overview**

The Visual Prompt Standards define how approved visual specifications are translated into consistent, high-quality prompts for image generation models.

The objective is to ensure every generated visual aligns with the organization's editorial style, maintains a consistent visual identity, and accurately communicates the intended message.

This guide does not determine **whether** a visual should be created or **which** visual pattern should be used. Those decisions are handled by the Visual Decision Framework, Visual Pattern Library, and Hero Image Guide.

This guide only defines **how visual specifications are converted into executable prompts.**

---

# **Guiding Principles**

Every generated prompt should prioritize:

* Consistency over creativity  
* Communication over decoration  
* Simplicity over complexity  
* Editorial quality over visual effects  
* Brand consistency over stylistic experimentation

Prompt generation should never invent concepts that are not present in the visual specification.

---

# **Visual Style System**

Two visual styles are supported.

---

# **Style A — Editorial Hero Images**

## **Purpose**

Used as the primary cover image for long-form content.

Hero images establish context, communicate the central idea of the article, and create an emotional connection before the reader begins reading.

---

## **Visual Characteristics**

Generate imagery that is:

* Editorial concept illustration  
* Painterly digital artwork  
* Semi-realistic  
* Premium editorial quality  
* Soft brush textures  
* Atmospheric lighting  
* Cinematic composition  
* Modern enterprise aesthetic  
* Human-centered where appropriate  
* Concept-driven rather than literal

The image should communicate a single visual idea.

---

## **Composition**

Hero images should follow a consistent composition.

* Wide landscape orientation  
* One dominant focal point  
* Strong visual hierarchy  
* Moderate environmental depth  
* Generous negative space  
* Balanced composition  
* Minimal visual clutter

---

## **Environment**

Use modern professional environments.

Examples include:

* Enterprise workplaces  
* Hybrid offices  
* Collaborative spaces  
* Abstract technology environments  
* Modern business settings

The environment should support the primary subject without competing for attention.

---

## **Lighting**

Preferred lighting:

* Soft natural lighting  
* Atmospheric lighting  
* Cinematic highlights  
* Balanced contrast

Avoid dramatic theatrical lighting unless explicitly required.

---

## **Color Direction**

Maintain the Eightfold visual identity.

Use:

* Neutral backgrounds  
* Warm orange accents  
* Blue supporting accents  
* Soft gradients  
* Clean enterprise palette

Accent colors should support the composition rather than dominate it.

---

## **Hero Image Constraints**

Never include:

* Readable text  
* Headlines  
* Logos  
* Watermarks  
* Screenshots  
* UI mockups  
* Charts  
* Graphs  
* Tables  
* Process diagrams  
* Excessive visual clutter

The Hero Image should tell a story through imagery alone.

---

# **Style B — Information Visuals**

## **Purpose**

Information visuals explain ideas that are difficult to communicate efficiently through text alone.

Their objective is comprehension rather than emotion.

---

## **Visual Characteristics**

Information visuals should be:

* Minimal  
* Clean  
* Structured  
* Data-focused  
* Highly readable  
* Enterprise presentation quality

Avoid decorative illustrations.

---

## **Design Principles**

Every information visual should:

* Communicate one idea  
* Reduce reading effort  
* Maintain strong hierarchy  
* Use generous whitespace  
* Support rapid scanning  
* Prioritize clarity over visual complexity

---

## **Layout System**

Approved layouts include:

* Cards  
* Grids  
* Columns  
* Rows  
* Timelines  
* Comparison layouts  
* Framework diagrams  
* Process flows  
* KPI summaries

Layouts should remain simple and predictable.

---

## **Typography**

Typography should establish hierarchy.

Preferred structure:

Headline

↓

Supporting statement

↓

Primary visual

↓

Labels

↓

Footnote

Keep text concise.

Information visuals should summarize rather than repeat article content.

---

## **Color System**

Maintain the Eightfold visual language.

Primary Accent

Eightfold Orange

Use for:

* Key metrics  
* Important numbers  
* Highlights  
* Primary emphasis

---

Secondary Accent

Eightfold Blue

Use for:

* Supporting information  
* Secondary emphasis  
* Labels  
* Navigation cues

---

Neutral Palette

Use:

* White  
* Light grey  
* Dark charcoal

These colors should form the majority of the composition.

---

## **Color Rules**

* Use one dominant accent color per visual.  
* Preserve high contrast for readability.  
* Avoid unnecessary gradients.  
* Use color to reinforce hierarchy rather than decoration.

---

## **Information Visual Constraints**

Avoid:

* Decorative illustrations  
* Complex backgrounds  
* Excessive icons  
* Multiple competing focal points  
* Long paragraphs  
* Unnecessary visual effects

Every element should contribute directly to understanding.

---

# **Prompt Construction**

Prompts should always follow the same structure.

## **Hero Images**

Generate prompts in the following order:

1. Visual Message  
2. Primary Subject  
3. Supporting Subject (if applicable)  
4. Environment  
5. Interaction  
6. Editorial Style  
7. Composition  
8. Lighting  
9. Color Direction  
10. Visual Constraints

---

## **Information Visuals**

Generate prompts in the following order:

1. Communication Goal  
2. Information Structure  
3. Visual Pattern  
4. Layout  
5. Hierarchy  
6. Color System  
7. Typography  
8. Constraints

---

# **Prompt Writing Principles**

Prompts should:

* Describe observable visual elements.  
* Remain concise and specific.  
* Avoid abstract marketing language.  
* Avoid repeating concepts.  
* Avoid explaining the article.  
* Avoid referencing internal processes or business context.  
* Focus on the desired visual outcome.

---

# **Standard Constraints**

All prompts should include:

* No readable text  
* No logos  
* No watermarks  
* No UI screenshots unless explicitly required  
* No unnecessary decorative elements  
* No cluttered compositions  
* No distorted anatomy  
* No unrealistic proportions  
* No low-quality rendering artifacts

## **Prompt Generation Principles**

Every prompt should follow these principles:

1. Follow the approved Eightfold visual language consistently.  
2. Generate visuals that communicate a single, clear idea.  
3. Maintain consistency across all content rather than introducing new artistic styles.  
4. Use composition, hierarchy, and whitespace to guide attention.  
5. Treat Hero Images as editorial storytelling assets and Information Visuals as explanatory assets.  
6. Ensure the visual can stand on its own without requiring additional explanation.  
7. Never generate text within the image unless explicitly requested as part of the design process.
